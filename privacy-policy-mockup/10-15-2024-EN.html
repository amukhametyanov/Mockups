There are instances when we collect your Personal Information from third parties or automatically, through the use of cookies and other technologies, such as when you visit our Website or make an online purchase.

2. Who Do We Share Your Information With?

We do not sell your Personal Information. In limited circumstances, we share your Personal Information with contractors, service providers, and other third parties we use to support our business such as delivery providers, analytics and search engine providers that assist us with Website improvement and optimization, and for personalization and advertising. When sharing your information, we take measures to limit who we share the information with and how much information we share to only what is necessary to achieve the purpose.

Specifically, we may disclose Personal Information that we collect or you provide to us under the following circumstances:

2.1. Affiliates And Service Providers:

We may share your Personal Information with our related companies and service providers who provide services for us. For example, we might need to approve and process your payments, host our Website, run certain features on our Website, show advertisements, send you emails or other messages, organize contests, promotions, fundraisers, or events, conduct customer research, and manage and analyze data and the effectiveness of our advertisements.

These companies and service providers will only have access to the Personal Information they need to do their jobs. We do not allow them to use or share your Personal Information for their own advertising or other purposes unless you have consented.

2.2. Legal Disclosures:

We may share your Personal Information (including transaction data, video footage, and images) with law enforcement and government agencies if required by a search warrant or other legally valid requests or inquiries. We might also share it with an investigative body in cases of alleged or actual agreement breaches, law violations, fraud, or loss prevention, as required by Canadian law. Additionally, we may disclose Personal Information if needed to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims, or as allowed by applicable law.

2.3. Mergers And Acquisitions:

We may transfer any information we have about you as an asset in connection with a merger or sale (including transfers made as part of insolvency or bankruptcy proceedings) involving all or part of Sporting Life or as part of a corporate reorganization or other change in corporate control.

2.4. Fraud Detection and Prevention:

When you make a purchase, we may share your Personal Information with our fraud prevention partner to help us detect and prevent fraudulent transactions. This Personal Information may include your first and last name, shipping and billing address, email address, partial payment card information, transaction-level details, as well as your IP address and other information about your device. Our fraud prevention partner uses the information we provide and combines it with other customer data they receive from their clients. For more information about the manner in which our fraud prevention partner manages your Personal Information, click here.

3. Where is Your Personal Information Stored?

We may process, store, access or transfer your Personal Information outside of the jurisdiction you reside in. For example, our service providers may be located outside of Quebec or Canada and your Personal Information may be subject to the laws of the jurisdiction in which it is stored, which may or may not be as comprehensive as the laws of the jurisdiction in which you reside. In these circumstances, the governments, courts, law enforcement, or regulatory agencies of the area of jurisdiction may be able to obtain access to your Personal Information through the applicable laws.

6. What Are Your Choices?

We created mechanisms to provide you with the following control over your information:

6.1. Tracking Technologies and Advertising:

We use cookies on our Website. “Cookies” are small text files which are saved on your computer’s browser when you visit a website. Cookies and other tracking technologies like web beacons/pixels and tags can collect information such as your browser type, IP address, device identifier and model, location information, and how you interact on our Website, such as the pages you visit, when you return to the Website, and the amount of time you view it. This information helps us manage our Website, improve your online shopping experience by saving your preferences, and suggesting relevant products we think you would like.

You have the choice to reject cookies by resetting your browser. You can manage cookies via your browser under your Settings. If you choose to decline cookies on your browser, you will reject cookies from all websites, not just ours. You may also lose some functionalities on our Website and other websites if your browser blocks all cookies.

Some content or applications on our Website, including advertisements, are also served by third parties, including advertisers, ad networks and servers, content providers, social media platforms and application providers. These third parties may use cookies to collect information about you when you use our Website, including information about your online activities over time and across different websites and other online services. They may use this information to provide you with interest-based (behavioural) advertising or other targeted content.

You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, or to alert you when cookies are being sent. To learn how you can manage your Flash cookie settings, visit the Flash player settings page on Adobe’s website.

6.2. Marketing Communications:

If you have signed up for marketing communications through a purchase (implied consent) or expressly opted in to receive communications from us, you can opt-out by:

  • “unsubscribing” directly from the email / SMS communication;
  • adjusting your user preferences directly in your SL Rewards profile; or
  • sending us an email (to privacyofficer@sportinglifegroup.ca) stating that you no longer wish to receive communications from us.

Once you have sent the unsubscribe request, please allow for 10 (ten) days for the request to be processed.

If you opt out of marketing communications, we may still send you communication regarding your product or service purchase, warranty registration, product service experience, or other transactions.

6.3. Interest-Based Advertising and Analytics:

Like many other websites, we use third parties, such as Meta and Google Analytics, for advertising and analytics. This allows us to understand how visitors use our Website, to promote interest-based advertisements to you, and to measure how users interact with those advertisements.

The information we collect through Google Analytics is aggregated and we cannot identify who you are. When we serve interest-based advertisements to you (via Meta or otherwise), we may do this using web tracking tools or by converting an email address, phone number or other contact information you provided to us into a unique value that these platforms may compare to a unique value created from information you provided to those platforms. These third parties use their own tracking technologies (e.g. cookies, beacons, pixel tags, flash cookies etc) on our Website and unaffiliated websites and social media to provide you with targeted advertising based on your interests.

You may opt out of the collection of your Personal Information by these third parties, including ad servers and networks by indicating your choice using the opt-out tool created by the Digital Advertising Alliance of Canada or an opt-out tool created by the Network Advertising Initiative. You can also access these websites to learn more about online behavioural advertising and how to stop websites from placing cookies on your device. Opting out of a network does not mean you will no longer receive online advertising. It does mean that the network from which you opted out will no longer deliver ads tailored to your web preferences and usage patterns. Please visit Facebook for information on Meta’s advertising and privacy policies https://www.facebook.com/about/privacy/.

7. How Do We Safeguard Your Personal Information?

We take every reasonable measure and precaution to protect and secure your Personal Information. We maintain administrative, technical and physical measures to safeguard your Personal Information under our custody and control against unauthorized access, use, modification and disclosure. Some of your Personal Information may be accessible online and protected by a password of your choice. We strongly recommend that you do not disclose your password to anyone. We will keep your Personal Information only as long as necessary to achieve the purposes for which it was collected, including meeting any legal, accounting, or reporting obligations, unless otherwise permitted or required by law. In some cases, we may anonymize your Personal Information so it can no longer be linked to you. We reserve the right to use this anonymous data for any legitimate business purpose without further notice to you or your consent.
